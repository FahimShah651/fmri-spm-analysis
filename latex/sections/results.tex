\subsection{Preprocessing Quality Control}

\textbf{Motion.}
Head motion was low in both datasets. Dataset~1 (184 volumes) had a
mean framewise displacement of roughly 0.15\,mm; Dataset~2 (121 volumes)
showed a similar profile (Fig.~\ref{fig:comparison:motion_qc}). Neither
run had the sustained high-motion episodes that would trigger volume
scrubbing. The six motion parameters per dataset are plotted in
Figs.~\ref{fig:ds1:motion_params} and~\ref{fig:ds2:motion_params}
(Methods). No volumes were censored.

\begin{table}[H]
  \centering
  \caption{Head Motion Quality Control --- Both Datasets}
  \label{tab:motion_qc}
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{4pt}
  \begin{tabular}{lcccc}
    \toprule
    Dataset  & Task   & Max Trans & Max Rot   & Mean FD \\
             &        & (mm)      & ($^\circ$)& (mm)    \\
    \midrule
    ds000114 & Motor  & $<$1.0    & $<$1.0    & $\sim$0.15 \\
    ds000105 & Visual & $<$1.2    & $<$1.0    & $\sim$0.18 \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

\textbf{Temporal SNR.}
Mean tSNR at mid-brain level was approximately 30 for Dataset~1 and
90 for Dataset~2 after preprocessing. The tSNR comparison and motion
traces are shown in Figs.~\ref{fig:comparison:motion_qc}
and~\ref{fig:comparison:tsnr}.

\begin{figure}[H]

\subsection{GLM Results --- Dataset 1 (Motor Task)}

Four contrasts were computed: Finger, Foot, and Lips each against
implicit baseline, plus an omnibus AllMotor contrast pooling all three.

\textbf{Individual motor contrasts.}
The Finger$\,{>}\,$Baseline map (Fig.~\ref{fig:ds1:tmap:finger}) shows
the expected bilateral hand-area cluster in the lateral central sulcus,
with left-hemisphere dominance for right-hand movement. Foot tapping
activated the superior medial wall bilaterally; lips movement produced
ventrolateral motor cortex activation with a secondary supplementary
motor area (SMA) cluster --- together, these three maps reproduce the
classical somatotopic organisation of the homunculus.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig13_ds1_tmap_finger_pos}
  \caption{Finger$\,{>}\,$Baseline t-map for Dataset~1
    ($p<0.001$ uncorr., $k>10$\,vox). Activated voxels concentrate
    in the hand area of primary motor cortex (lateral central sulcus)
    and ipsilateral cerebellum, consistent with right-hand movement.}
  \label{fig:ds1:tmap:finger}
\end{figure}

\textbf{All-motor omnibus.}
Pooling all three conditions (Fig.~\ref{fig:ds1:tmap:allmotor}) reveals
the full extent of the voluntary motor system: the entire motor strip
from the medial foot territory to the lateral face territory, plus the
cerebellum and thalamus.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig16_ds1_tmap_allmotor_pos}
  \caption{All-motor omnibus contrast (Finger\,+\,Foot\,+\,Lips,
    each weighted $1/3$) for Dataset~1. Activation spans the full
    motor strip from foot (medial) to face (lateral) territory plus
    the cerebellum and basal ganglia.}
  \label{fig:ds1:tmap:allmotor}
\end{figure}

\subsection{GLM Results --- Dataset 2 (Visual Task)}

\textbf{Face selectivity.}
The face$\,{>}\,$Baseline contrast (Fig.~\ref{fig:ds2:tmap:face}) produced
a right-lateralised cluster in the fusiform gyrus --- the fusiform face
area (FFA) identified by Kanwisher et al. Replicating this well-known
result with an independent pipeline validates the preprocessing and GLM.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig19_ds2_tmap_face_pos}
  \caption{Faces$\,{>}\,$Baseline t-map for Dataset~2. The dominant
    cluster in the right fusiform gyrus corresponds to the FFA.
    Additional occipital activation reflects low-level visual
    responses shared across all image categories.}
  \label{fig:ds2:tmap:face}
\end{figure}

\textbf{All-visual omnibus.}
Pooling all eight visual categories (Fig.~\ref{fig:ds2:tmap:allvisual})
reveals the ventral visual stream from V1/V2 through the fusiform and
parahippocampal gyri. Object-category contrasts produced distributed
lateral occipito-temporal activation consistent with Haxby
et~al.~\cite{Haxby2001}.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig22_ds2_tmap_allvisual_pos}
  \caption{All-visual omnibus contrast for Dataset~2 (mean of all
    8 category regressors). The entire ventral visual stream from
    V1 through the fusiform and parahippocampal gyri is recruited.}
  \label{fig:ds2:tmap:allvisual}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Functional Connectivity}

\textbf{Dataset~1 --- Motor Network.}
The left M1 seed produced strong positive correlations
(Fig.~\ref{fig:ds1:connectivity}) in bilateral M1, SMA, premotor
cortex, and thalamus --- the classic sensorimotor resting-state
network of Biswal et~al.~\cite{Biswal1995}.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{figures/fig21_ds1_connectivity_M1_hand}
  \caption{DS1 seed-based connectivity (left M1, MNI
    $[-38,-26,60]$\,mm, 6\,mm sphere). Warm: $r>0.30$
    sensorimotor network. Cool: anticorrelated default-mode regions.}
  \label{fig:ds1:connectivity}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Dataset~2 --- Visual Network.}
The V1 seed (Fig.~\ref{fig:ds2:connectivity}) produced strong
correlations throughout the occipital lobe, fusiform gyrus, and
dorsal parietal cortex. Default-mode regions were anti-correlated.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{figures/fig22_ds2_connectivity_V1_calcarine}
  \caption{DS2 seed-based connectivity (V1 calcarine,
    MNI $[0,-88,2]$\,mm, 6\,mm sphere). Warm: occipital and ventral
    visual network ($r>0.30$). Cool: default-mode suppression.}
  \label{fig:ds2:connectivity}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Cross-Dataset Comparison}

Figure~\ref{fig:comparison:connectivity} shows both connectivity maps
at the same axial slice: the motor seed activates central and frontal
regions while the visual seed is confined to occipital and temporal
areas. Figure~\ref{fig:comparison:activation} shows the corresponding
t-maps. Both double dissociations confirm that the pipeline produces
anatomically meaningful, system-specific results.

    Warm = $r>0.30$; cool = negative. The two networks are
    spatially non-overlapping.}
